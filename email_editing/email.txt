Justin and I have been discussing how to best execute data engineering on the Kiewit Data Platform (KDP). To move forward effectively, we need to sequence data sources by domain and build data models for each vertical. Currently, we are not doing this—and progress has been slow as a result.

The present state of KDP includes nearly 30 Snowflake databases with significant sprawl: limited data models at scale, duplication, and fragmented ownership. While there are horizontal dependencies across pipelines, the higher priority is proper build-out of data models and pipelines within each domain.

To be effective, a Domain Engineering Manager must:
- Understand the source systems within their domain
- Manage and monitor their pipelines end-to-end
- Enable downstream consumers to provide feedback not just via tickets, but through code—pull requests, observability, and data quality checks

This is fundamentally different from how we have operated for the past 15 years, which produced the sprawl. The key is deep domain expertise at Kiewit combined with daily collaboration with consumers to integrate workloads.

**Request:** We recommend hiring three Data Engineering Managers who can utilize the ARB-recommended tools and own their respective domains. I am seeking your approval to open these three requisitions.

Happy to discuss any element of this.
