KIEWIT InEight SOFTWARE - MARKET CAP ANALYSIS

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Total Annual Market Value of InEight Usage: $122,686,067
Range: $106,460,032 - $138,912,103
Note: Pay rates increased by 15% from original estimates

This represents the total annual cost of staff time spent using InEight software
across Kiewit's enterprise. Any efficiency improvements that reduce InEight usage
time would directly save this amount proportionally.

METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. User Base: 3,127 InEight users (from Cumberland proxy analysis)
   - Field Engineers: 1,688 (54%)
   - Project Engineers: 83 (3%)
   - Superintendents: 1,356 (43%)

2. Hours Calculation:
   - Each person works 2,080 hours/year
   - Each person spends 25% of work time in InEight
   - Total InEight hours: 1,626,040 hours/year

3. Cost Calculation:
   - Used all-in hourly rates (includes 48% burden)
   - Field Engineer: $57.34 - $72.48/hour (15% increase)
   - Project Engineer: $68.33 - $88.86/hour (15% increase)
   - Superintendent: $75.42 - $101.34/hour (15% increase)

4. Module Allocation:
   - Based on validated InEight software documentation
   - Allocated hours by role to specific modules
   - All percentages sum to 100% for each role

KEY INSIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Superintendents represent the highest cost ($62.3M) despite being fewer
   in number than Field Engineers, due to higher hourly rates.

2. Plan module is the highest value target ($39.3M) for efficiency improvements,
   representing 32% of total cost.

3. Field Engineers use the most hours (877,760) but at lower rates, resulting
   in $57.0M total cost.

4. Project Engineers, though small in number (83), have the highest per-user
   cost ($40,869/user) due to high hourly rates and heavy software usage.

5. The total market cap of $122.7M (with 15% salary increase) represents 
   significant opportunity for AI-driven efficiency gains.
