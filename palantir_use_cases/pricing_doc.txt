Palantir Use Cases - Kiewit Implementation

1. KIEWIT CORE ONTOLOGY + AI AGENTS
    Core Ontology Development including any new relevant data sources
    Construction Ontology and AI Agents
    Engineering Ontology and AI Agents
    Procurement Ontology and AI Agents
    Conversational agents and small automations (2 included)

2. CENTRALIZED TAKEOFF
    PDF/Drawing Quantity Extraction, Standardization and Material Ontology mapping
    3D Model Quantity Extraction, Standardization and Material Ontology mapping
    📌 NOTE: Centralized Takeoff + Estimate 360 together = design ingestion → Kiewit's takeoff vision models → estimate quantity product from partial model design.

3. ESTIMATE 360
    Estimate 360: centralized view across Construction, Engineering and Procurement
    Construction Estimate: Takeoff to InEight Estimate mapping and connection
    Engineering Estimate: RFP (PDF) / Takeoff → Engineering Scope
    Procurement Estimate: Takeoff → Procurement Package → Line item

4. SCHEDULING
    Construction Schedule: WBS, structures, activities, durations, dependencies
    Engineering Schedule: design phase schedules with appropriate milestones and dependencies
    Procurement Schedule: procurement milestones, vendor selection timelines, material delivery scheduling
        📌 NOTE: Procurement Schedule = add-on to Source 360 (supplier quote comparison tool Palantir is already building for Kiewit).
    Integrated Master Schedule: cross-discipline schedule integration, dependencies
    💡 OPPORTUNITY: Construction Equipment Scheduling could be high-value — Kiewit spends $1B/year on construction equipment.

5. EXECUTION
    Takeoff → Work Planning
    Initial Work Package Generation
    Short Interval Planning Generation

6. RESOURCE MANAGEMENT
    Category management and cost procurement
    Project digital cockpit (PDC)

7. VENDOR PERFORMANCE
    Vendor Recommendation
    Vendor Health


CONCERNS
━━━━━━━━

1. Go-forward pricing beyond Year 1 is a real concern — no de-escalation in cost is coming.

2. We have been getting scoping for free because the engineers on the account are still tied to delivery of Source 360 or TRACE and are doing scoping on the side.

3. The workflow is somewhat chronological — all downstream work depends on model quantities and components being in place first.
