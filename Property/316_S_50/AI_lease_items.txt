A well-written residential lease in Omaha, Nebraska should clearly outline the rights and responsibilities of both the landlord and tenant, adhering to the Nebraska Uniform Residential Landlord and Tenant Act and federal fair housing laws. 
Key components to include are:
I. Parties and Property Identification
Identification of Parties: Full legal names and contact information for all tenants and the landlord or authorized property manager.
Property Description: The exact address and description of the rental unit, including any shared areas, parking, or storage facilities included. 
II. Financial Terms
Rent Details: The exact monthly rent amount, the due date, acceptable payment methods, and the location for payment.
Late Fees and Bounced Checks: Specific details on any late fees (Nebraska law does not cap these, but they should be reasonable) and returned check fees (limited to $10 plus any bank fees).
Security Deposit: The amount of the security deposit, which cannot exceed one month's rent for unfurnished properties (an additional pet deposit not exceeding a quarter of one month's rent is also allowed). The lease should also specify the conditions for its return and that an itemized list of deductions will be provided within 14 days of move-out.
Utilities: A clear breakdown of which utilities are included in the rent and which are the tenant's responsibility. 
III. Tenancy Details and Logistics
Lease Term and Renewal: The start and end dates of the lease, and the procedures and notice periods required for renewal or non-renewal (e.g., a 30-day written notice is required to terminate a month-to-month lease).
Occupancy and Guests: The names of all adult occupants and rules regarding long-term guests or unauthorized occupants.
Use of Property: Specific rules and regulations regarding the use of the unit and common areas (e.g., no smoking policy, noise levels, pet rules, parking guidelines).
Subletting/Assignment: Whether subletting is permitted and the process for obtaining landlord consent. 
IV. Maintenance and Legal Compliance
Maintenance and Repairs: Explicitly defined responsibilities for both landlord and tenant (landlord generally handles major repairs and habitability, tenant handles minor upkeep like changing light bulbs). The procedure for submitting repair requests, including for emergencies, must be outlined.
Landlord Entry: The requirement for the landlord to provide at least 24 hours' notice before entry for non-emergency reasons and only at reasonable times.
Required Disclosures:
Lead-Based Paint: A disclosure for any property built before 1978, as required by federal law.
Landlord/Agent Identification: The name and address of the property manager and the owner or authorized person for receiving official notices.
Prohibited Provisions: Assurance that the lease does not contain clauses prohibited by the Nebraska Uniform Residential Landlord and Tenant Act (e.g., waiver of tenant rights, agreement to pay the landlord's attorney's fees). 
V. Termination and Dispute Resolution
Termination Clause: The procedures and penalties for early lease termination, ensuring compliance with Nebraska's legal framework for evictions and notice periods.
Signatures: Signatures and dates from all adult tenants and the landlord for the agreement to be legally binding. 
For best practice, both parties should conduct a move-in inspection and document the property's condition with a signed checklist and photos to avoid disputes over damages later.
